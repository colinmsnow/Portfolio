Braille Printer

Robotics, Accessability

Creating a low-cost and easy to use braille printer that fits with the lives of those who use it.

Even at the low end, braille printers often cost upwards of $2000, and this high entry cost makes it hard for anyone to supply braille as an alternative to printed materials. Throughout this project, we worked with VIBUG, the visually impaired and blind user group in Boston, to develop a printer that was fast, easy to use, and portable enough to travel around to wherever it is needed.

Please check out our project website to see all the details including our design process and the finished product.
