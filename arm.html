Robot Arm

Controls, Robotics

An ambitious project to make a 7-axis robot arm that has only just begun.

There is almost nothing in robotics that says difficult and expensive like a milti-axis robot arm. But, as in many times before, all these warnings went unheeded as we set out to make one ourselves. Because, after all, nothing in robotics says cool like a robot arm.

Actuator

In order to keep things to a slightly more reasonable scope, we are beginning by making a precision robotic actuator for the arm so that when it comes time to assembling it we will simply need to link the joints together. The actuator will be driven by a brushless motor, and will have a cycloidal gearbox to get a powerful gear ratio with no backlash.

We have designed and started working on the actuator, but our progress has been slowed by losing access to the shops and electrical prototyping spaces when we were all sent home. We still plan to finish the electrical systems by the end of the semester, and get started right away on building the actuator next semester. Here is our proposal for work this semester with explanations of all the subsystems.

We have also designed a brushless motor controller which is able to supply 30V 20A power to each actuator and move it at a precise speed.

Arm Simulation

While building the actuator, we have also been working on simulations of the arm and inverse kinematics so that it can move to specified coordinates automatically. We are working in PyBullet with a sample model of a similar arm, and are also building our own 3D arm model so that all the kinematics are accurate. This model is also helping to inform how we create the actuator, as we can simulate loads and accelerations before we build it.
